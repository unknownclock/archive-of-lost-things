What Is a Lost Thing?

A lost thing is any object that is no longer in your possession — intentionally or unintentionally — and whose location is unknown. You can’t find it. You likely never will. And yet… it still exists for you.

That presence in absence is what matters here.

Your lost thing might live on as:

  • A vivid memory
  • A blurry photo
  • A sketch or drawing
  • A version or replica

What matters most is the feeling of the thing — and the story it carries.

How to Submit

Fill out the submission form.

  • Describe your object — in writing and/or a voice recording.
  • If you have a visual (photo, drawing, or version), upload it.
  • If your object exists only in memory, describe it in detail.

Voice recordings are encouraged — your voice brings it to life.

What to Include

Please describe:

  • What the object looked like
  • What it meant to you
  • The story of how you got it
  • The story of how you lost it
  • What memories or emotions are tied to it
“Objects are never just objects. They are doors that open to free our emotions. They are material things that contain memories — even a tiny part — of who we are and what we’ve lived.”
